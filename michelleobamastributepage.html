Michelle LaVaughn Robinson Obama

Former first African American Lady of the United States from 2009 to 2017

"[Y]our success will be determined by your own confidence and fortitude." - Michelle Obama

Accomplishments

  • 1964:  Born in Chicago, IL to Fraser C. and Marian Shields Robinson III
  • 1985:  Graduated with a Bacheloar of Arts from Princeton University, Cum Laude
  • 1988:  Graduate from Harvard Law School with a Juris Doctor (J.D.) degree.
  • 1991:  Assistant to the Mayor, and as Assistant Commissioner of Planning and Development
  • 1992:  Married U.S. President, Barack Obama and raised two daughters.
  • 1993:  Executive Director for the Chicago office of Public Allies
  • 2007:  Campainged for her husband's presidential bid throughout 2007 and 2008
  • 2008:  Delivered a keynote address at the 2008 Democratic National Convention
  • 2012:  Spoke for her husband at Democratic National Convention
  • 2016:  Delivered a speech at the Democratic National Convention in Philadelphia in support of Hillary Clinton.
